# 🎨 MUSE: The Eraser Strikes
### Teacher Guide & Reference Manual
**M.U.S.E. — Masterwork Unity & Salvage Enforcement**

---

## What Is This?

A standalone, browser-based art education game for middle school students built around a time-travel mystery. A villain called **The Eraser** is stealing famous paintings from history, and students play as rookie art detectives recruited to recover them.

**No installation. No login. No internet account required for the game UI.**
Open `index.html` in any browser and play.

**Estimated play time:** 60–75 minutes for all 6 missions

---

## The Story

> *"An unknown villain called THE ERASER is traveling through time, stealing famous paintings from history. Every painting they erase creates a timeline glitch. Left unchecked? Art history unravels. No Renaissance. No Impressionism. No ukiyo-e. Just blank walls everywhere."*

Students are guided by **PIXEL** — a sarcastic, funny AI assistant who delivers clues in short punchy typewriter lines, reacts to wrong answers with humor, and celebrates correct ones enthusiastically. The Eraser is never seen — only their snarky calling cards left at each crime scene.

## One Mission — Step by Step (~10 Minutes Each)

```
1. ARRIVE        PIXEL delivers 3 funny lines introducing the era
                 Typewriter effect, short and punchy

2. THE SMUDGE    Empty frame where the painting was stolen
                 The Eraser left a snarky calling card note
                 "Timeline destabilizing..."

3. CLUE 1        PIXEL's first riddle about the missing painting
                 Observation-based — visual details, not memorized facts

4. SUSPECT       3 paintings appear as a lineup
   LINEUP        Press 1, 2, or 3 on keyboard — OR click/tap

5. WRONG?        PIXEL reacts with humor. The Eraser mocks you.
                 A second more specific clue unlocks automatically.

6. RIGHT!        Celebration! "PAINTING RECOVERED! TIMELINE RESTORED!"
                 PIXEL is very pleased with you.

7. DEEP DIVE     The real painting appears large
                 4 wild facts delivered as PIXEL speech bubbles
                 Students tap/click through like a comic strip

8. STAMP         Era stamp added to Art Detective Passport
                 PIXEL delivers a closing one-liner
                 Back to the mission map — one more stamp filled in
```

## PA State Standards Alignment

| Standard | Description | How It's Addressed |
|----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **9.1.8.A** | Production — materials & processes | Deep Dive facts cover medium, technique, and process for each work |
| **9.1.8.B** | Production — elements & principles | Clue riddles require reading visual elements: color, light, composition |
| **9.1.8.C** | Production — vocabulary | Pointillism, ukiyo-e, fresco, sfumato — embedded in clues and facts |
| **9.2.8.A** | Historical & Cultural Contexts | Era briefings + facts place every work in historical context |
| **9.2.8.B** | Chronological relationships | Mission map spans 5,000 years of art history across 6 eras |
| **9.2.8.C** | Art movements & influences | Cross-cultural connections: Japanese prints → Impressionism, etc. |
| **9.3.8.A** | Critical Response — analysis | Students must analyze visual clues to identify each stolen painting |
| **9.3.8.B** | Critical Response — judgment | Wrong answers include reasoning about why a painting doesn't fit the clue |

**The core alignment:** Critical visual observation IS the gameplay mechanic. Students cannot guess randomly — they must read clues and reason their way to each answer.

### Option 1 — Google Classroom
1. Open Teacher Panel → **💾 DOWNLOAD STANDALONE HTML**
2. Upload the `.html` file to Google Drive
3. Share the Drive link in Google Classroom
4. Students open it in Chrome — works immediately, no install

## Sample Facts Students Encounter

Facts are designed to be genuinely surprising — not textbook summaries:

- 🕵️ The Mona Lisa was **stolen from the Louvre in 1911** by a handyman who hid overnight and walked out with it tucked under his coat. It was missing for 2 years.
- 🐒 Seurat's La Grande Jatte has a **fashionable pet monkey** in it — on a leash, in a Paris park, in 1886.
- 🔍 Girl with a Pearl Earring's earring is probably **painted glass, not a real pearl** — and nobody knows who she was.
- 💀 Hokusai said on his **deathbed at age 90**: *"If only heaven gave me 10 more years... then I could become a real painter."*
- 📜 Some ancient Egyptians were buried with scrolls that still had the **name blank** — they paid for the spell package but forgot to fill in the form.
- 🎭 Tanner painted The Banjo Lesson **as a direct response** to art that mocked or caricatured Black Americans. He made the most tender family scene he could imagine, on purpose.

## Classroom Activities

### Before Playing
- Show the mission map — ask which era students are most curious about and why
- Quick discussion: "What would an art thief steal, and why would they take it?"

### During
- Encourage reading clues carefully before choosing — reward thinking out loud
- If projecting for the class: reveal clues one at a time and vote before clicking

### After Completing
- Which painting surprised you most? Which fact was hardest to believe?
- **Writing prompt:** Write a ransom note from The Eraser defending why they stole one painting
- **Research extension:** Find one more fact about your favorite recovered painting
- **Cross-curricular:** Connect Harlem Renaissance to Great Migration (social studies), Hokusai's wave to physics of waves (science), Egyptian afterlife to religion/mythology

### Assessment Ideas
- Screenshot or print the final **Art Detective Passport** as a completion record
- Use the PA Standard tags in each Deep Dive as exit ticket prompts
- Ask students to explain WHY one of their wrong-answer decoys wasn't the stolen painting

## Progress Saving

- Saves automatically to the **browser's localStorage**
- Students who close and reopen `index.html` in the same Chrome browser pick up where they left off
- Progress does **not** sync between different computers or browsers
- Teacher Panel → Reset clears all progress for a fresh start

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Fix |
|---------|-----|
| Image shows placeholder | Wikimedia may be slow — wait a moment or refresh the page |
| Game won't open | Use Chrome or Firefox — not Internet Explorer or very old browsers |
| Progress not saving | Open from a local file path, not from an email attachment |
| Pixel font looks wrong | Requires internet for Google Fonts — check connection |
| Forgot teacher PIN | Open the HTML in a text editor and search for `pin:` — it's stored in plain text in the JavaScript |
